# Salinised Soils
Salinised soils are a form of land degradation where soluble salts accumulate in the soil profile, often due to improper irrigation or as a secondary impact of disasters. This process reduces soil fertility and agricultural productivity. Disasters, such as tsunamis or storm surges, can contribute to ecosystem degradation and lead to the salinisation of soils, particularly in coastal agricultural areas.
