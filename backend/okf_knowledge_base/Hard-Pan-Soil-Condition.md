# Hard Pan (Soil Condition)"
A compacted layer within the soil profile that restricts root growth and water infiltration. In [[Conservation Agriculture]] practices, such as those in Namibia, a tractor-drawn ripper-furrower is used to rip the hard pan to a depth of 60 centimeters. This action improves water harvesting by concentrating water in the root zone and enhances soil structure, allowing for better root penetration.
