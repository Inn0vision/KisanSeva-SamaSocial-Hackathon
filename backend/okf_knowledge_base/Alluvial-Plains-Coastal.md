# Alluvial Plains (Coastal)
Coastal alluvial plains, including major deltas like the Nile, Red River, Ganges/Brahmaputra, and Mekong, are agricultural sub-systems highly vulnerable to climate change impacts. They face threats from sea level rise, salinization of aquifers and estuaries, increased frequency of cyclones, and increased frequency and intensity of floods. Adaptive options include minimizing infrastructure development, better conjunctive use of surface water and groundwater, integrated flood management plans, and improved management of coastal aquifers.
