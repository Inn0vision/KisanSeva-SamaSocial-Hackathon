# Cover Crops
Cover crops are plants grown primarily to protect and enrich the soil, rather than for harvest. They are crucial for optimizing soil cover, reducing runoff and soil erosion, and enhancing soil structure. Examples given are [[Radish]] and [[Pigeon Peas]] for breaking up compacted soils. They also take up nitrogen, reducing its loss from the soil, and are part of conservation agriculture and integrated soil-crop-water management.
