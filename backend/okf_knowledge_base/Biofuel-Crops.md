# Biofuel Crops
Biofuel crops are plants specifically cultivated for the production of biofuels (solid, liquid, or gaseous forms of energy). The text discusses their potential contribution to Climate-Smart Agriculture (CSA) objectives, including improving access to modern energy services and strengthening resilience to climate change. However, it also highlights complexities regarding their impact on GHG emissions and carbon sequestration, emphasizing the need for full life cycle assessments and careful management (e.g., agroecological zoning to avoid high carbon areas like peat land) to prevent negative impacts on food security or the environment.
