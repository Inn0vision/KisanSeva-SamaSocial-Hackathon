# Cation Exchange Capacity (CEC)
Cation Exchange Capacity (CEC) is a measure of a soil's ability to retain positively charged ions (cations), which include essential plant nutrients like potassium, calcium, and magnesium. The negative charges on the surfaces of [[Clay Soils]] particles and [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]] attract these cations, providing a nutrient reserve available to plant roots. Soils with higher clay content and SOM generally have a higher CEC, indicating greater potential fertility. This property is crucial for nutrient availability and is one of the soil properties most important with regard to climate change.
