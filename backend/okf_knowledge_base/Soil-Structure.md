# Soil Structure
Soil structure refers to the arrangement of individual soil particles (sand, silt, and clay) into aggregates, and the pore spaces between them. It is a crucial soil property that influences water infiltration, aeration, and the soil's capacity to retain water. Unlike [[Soil Texture]], soil structure can be modified by practices such as tillage or traffic. Improving soil structure with [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]] is a key principle for climate change adaptation and mitigation, as it enhances the soil's ability to store water and sequester carbon. Mechanical soil disturbance (e.g., ploughing) is detrimental to the build-up of SOM and the stabilization of aggregates.
