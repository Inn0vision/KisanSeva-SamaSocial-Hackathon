# Red Tide
A condition of excessive algae production (algal bloom) due to excessive nutrient availability in the water. It is increasingly associated with climate change and climatic variability. Red tides can lead to hypoxia (low oxygen levels) and losses of biodiversity. They are a common climate-related threat to mussel farming and other bivalve aquaculture, as they can contaminate shellfish with toxins, posing a health risk. The best approach for reducing exposure is the implementation of permanent food safety monitoring programmes.
