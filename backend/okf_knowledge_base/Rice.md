# Rice
A major global food crop, often cultivated in flooded conditions (paddy), which contributes to methane (CH4) emissions. Rice production is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts; for example, India experienced a 23% decrease due to drought, and Indonesia suffered significant losses from flooding.

The System of Rice Intensification (SRI) is a set of farming practices developed to increase the productivity of land, water, and other resources for rice cultivation. SRI focuses on developing healthy, large, and deep-root systems that can better resist drought, waterlogging, and rainfall variability. It requires intermittent water application instead of continuous flood irrigation, leading to significant yield increases (17-105%) and reduced water requirements (24-50%). SRI has also been shown to reduce the need for chemical fertilizers and minimize insect or disease infestation.
