# Seabed mollusc culture
A traditional form of mollusc aquaculture conducted in a wide range of coastal areas. It can be artisanal or commercial, with some operations being mechanized. Production is for household consumption or markets. The system is susceptible to climate change impacts such as changes in productivity, substrate quality, spatfall, and interactions with disease and predators. Response options include adjusting seeding and harvesting locations, improving health monitoring, and managing substrates.
