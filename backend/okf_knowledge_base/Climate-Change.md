# Climate Change
A significant threat to mussel farming and aquaculture in general. Climate-related threats include changes in water quality (e.g., temperature and salinity), increased frequency and prevalence of red tides, ocean acidification, and extreme weather events. These factors can affect mussel growth, seed availability, and overall production viability. Adaptation measures include monitoring programs, selecting resistant strains, and using hatcheries.
