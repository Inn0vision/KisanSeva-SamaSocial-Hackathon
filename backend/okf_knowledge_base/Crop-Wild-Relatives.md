# Crop Wild Relatives
Approximately 50,000 to 60,000 species that serve as potential gene donors for crop improvement. They represent a crucial source of genetic diversity, offering traits that can enhance the resilience and adaptability of cultivated crops to environmental challenges, including [[Pests]], [[Diseases]], and extreme climatic conditions.

# Crop Wild Relatives
Crop wild relatives are valuable genetic resources for food and agriculture, playing an integral part in agricultural and food production systems. Their maintenance and evolution depend on continued human intervention and traditional knowledge. *In situ* conservation efforts often focus on protecting sites where important populations of these target species are present, designating them as genetic reserves. They are crucial for providing adaptive traits for agriculture to cope with climate change.
