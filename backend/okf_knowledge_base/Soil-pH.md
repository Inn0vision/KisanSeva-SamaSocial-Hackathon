# Soil pH
Soil pH is a measure of the acidity or alkalinity of the soil, ranging from 4 to 7 for acidic soils and 7 to 9 for alkaline soils. It significantly influences [[Soil Nutrients]] availability and [[Soil Biological Activity]]. More fertile soils typically have a pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Acidity reduces bacterial activity, impacting the decomposition of [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]] and nutrient release, and can hinder the growth of [[Legumes]] and nitrogen-fixing _Rhizobia_. Strongly alkaline soils also suppress biological activity, reduce nutrient availability, and are at risk of SOM decomposition, [[Soil Salinization]], soil crusting, and accumulation of toxic minerals.
