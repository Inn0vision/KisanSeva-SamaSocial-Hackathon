# Forest Tree Species
Comprising over 80,000 species, these trees are home to over 80% of terrestrial biodiversity. They provide fundamental genetic diversity for evolution and adaptation to changing and adverse conditions, including climate change. They play a crucial role in mitigating climate change through [[Carbon sequestration]] and provide livelihood benefits in agro-forestry systems. Less than one percent of these species have been studied in depth for their potential.

# Forest Tree Species
Forest tree species are characterized as long-lived, highly genetically diverse organisms with natural mechanisms for maintaining high levels of intraspecific variation, such as wide dispersal of pollen and seeds. These mechanisms, combined with diverse native environments, have contributed to their evolution into some of the most genetically variable organisms on Earth. Conservation efforts for forest species often use the term *circa situm* (or *circa situ*) to refer to conservation within altered agricultural landscapes like agroforestry systems and home gardens, outside their natural habitat but within their native geographical range.
