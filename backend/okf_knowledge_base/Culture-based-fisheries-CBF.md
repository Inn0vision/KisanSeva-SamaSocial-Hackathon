# Culture-based fisheries (CBF)
A form of aquaculture involving perennial and seasonal stock enhancement practiced in smaller water bodies that cannot support a fishery through natural recruitment. Stocked fish are managed communally with ownership rights. It is considered an environmentally friendly food production system with a small environmental footprint, as it does not consume water or external feed resources; the primary input is seed stock. Yields are lower than intensive aquaculture but it is cost-effective and has no feed-related GHG contribution. CBF is sensitive to climate change, particularly changes in rainfall patterns that affect water levels for stocking and harvesting. Adaptation strategies include staggered harvesting, coordinated marketing, and improved water management planning.
