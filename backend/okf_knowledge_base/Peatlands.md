# Peatlands
Peatlands are ecosystems characterized by a substantial layer of organic matter near the surface, making them a type of [[Organic Soils]]. Most pristine peatlands are wet during most of the year and contain 30 percent of the world's [[Soil Carbon]]. Draining peatlands for agriculture or forestry leads to significant GHG emissions and [[Soil Degradation]]. Rewetting drained peatlands and practicing paludiculture are key approaches for reducing emissions and providing benefits.

# Peatlands
Peatlands are a specific type of soil, rich in organic matter, that are significant hotspots of carbon emissions when converted to cropland or plantations. Draining peat soils for commercial production systems in tropical environments causes ongoing losses of up to 25 tonnes of carbon per hectare per year. Boreal peatlands also emit carbon when cultivated, though at a lower rate. They are particularly vulnerable to climate change and require specific attention in land use planning.
