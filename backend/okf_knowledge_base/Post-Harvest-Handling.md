# Post-Harvest Handling
The stage in the value chain encompassing activities after harvest or landing, including storage, preservation, and initial processing. A major source of food loss, particularly in developing countries. Causes of loss include spoilage, spillage, contamination, degradation, and inadequate cooling. Good practices involve careful handling to avoid mechanical damage, immediate transportation, and the use of improved storage and processing techniques like solar drying.
