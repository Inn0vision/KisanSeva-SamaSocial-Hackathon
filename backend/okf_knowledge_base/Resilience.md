# Resilience
Although often limited to the ability of a system to ‘bounce back’ to its previous state when faced with a shock, in the context of climate-smart agriculture, it involves efforts to improve the ecological, social, and economic well-being of the system before and after shocks. The concept is expanded to "building back better," which reflects the need not only to respond to emergencies but to put in place disaster risk management that better prepares for the next potential emergency. For fisheries and aquaculture, building resilience involves decreasing the exposure of the sector, decreasing human communities' sensitivities to change, and increasing the sector's adaptive capacity.

# Resilience
The capacity of individuals, households, communities, and systems to anticipate, absorb, accommodate, or recover from the effects of a shock or stress in a timely and efficient manner. In the context of agriculture and climate change, it refers to the ability to maintain productivity and wellbeing in the face of climate-related shocks. Safety nets are explicitly designed to protect and build this resilience, which is a central pillar of Climate-Smart Agriculture.
