# Degraded Soils
Degraded soils are those that have lost their productive capacity and are at much greater risk from the damaging impacts of climate change. They are vulnerable due to serious losses of [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]] and [[Soil Biodiversity]], increased [[Soil Compaction]], and higher rates of [[Soil Erosion]] and landslides. Land degradation itself is identified as a major cause of climate change. Restoring degraded soils is a key strategy for climate change resilience.

# Degraded Soils
Degraded soils are those that have suffered serious losses of Soil Organic Matter (SOM) and soil biodiversity, greater soil compaction, and increased rates of soil erosion and landslides. They are at much greater risk from the damaging impacts of climate change. Land degradation, including that of soils, is a major cause of climate change, accounting for a significant portion of human-induced GHG emissions. Restoration of degraded soils, particularly through increasing SOM content and reducing erosion, holds the highest potential for carbon sequestration.

# Degraded Soils
Soils that have lost their fertility, structure, or overall health, often due to decades of intensive cropping. This degradation leads to nutrient deficiencies and makes crops more susceptible to [[Soil-borne Pests]] and [[Soil-borne Diseases]]. Land degradation and soil erosion result in lower yields and loss of arable land. The restoration of degraded lands is recognized as an important practice for climate change mitigation.
