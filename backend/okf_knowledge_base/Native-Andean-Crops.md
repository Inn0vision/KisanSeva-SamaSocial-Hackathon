# Native Andean Crops
Native Andean crops refer to a variety of traditional crops cultivated in the Andean region, which are central to indigenous biocultural heritage. These crops, including numerous potato varieties, are adapted to local conditions and represent a vital source of genetic diversity. Conservation efforts, such as the establishment of Indigenous Biocultural Heritage Areas like the Potato Park, aim to protect these crops and the associated traditional knowledge from the impacts of climate change and biodiversity loss.
