# Land Conversion
Land conversion refers to the change in land use, such as converting natural ecosystems (e.g., grasslands, forests, peatlands) to agricultural land (cropland or plantations). The text highlights that intensive land uses are expanding into areas where [[Soil Organic Carbon (SOC)]] stocks are less resilient, leading to significant losses of SOC. Examples include the conversion of semi-arid savannas, grasslands, tropical rainforests, and peatlands, which are identified as hotspots of carbon emissions. Preventing land conversion and protecting vulnerable lands from high [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]] losses is a key strategy for climate-smart land use.
