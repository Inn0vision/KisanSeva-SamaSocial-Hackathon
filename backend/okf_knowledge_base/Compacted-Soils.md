# Compacted Soils
Compacted soils are characterized by reduced airspaces and decreased penetration for plant roots, restricting the growth of lateral roots and fine root hairs important for moisture and nutrient uptake. They are seriously affected by dry spells. Compaction is caused by repetitive hoeing, ploughing, heavy machinery, or livestock trampling. Subsoiling can break up compacted layers, and prevention measures like controlled traffic systems, minimum tillage, and cover crops are crucial.
