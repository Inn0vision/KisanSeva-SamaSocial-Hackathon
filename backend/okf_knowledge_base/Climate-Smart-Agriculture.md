# Climate-Smart Agriculture
An integrative approach to agricultural development that aims to simultaneously achieve three outcomes: increased productivity and incomes, enhanced resilience and adaptation to climate change, and reduced greenhouse gas emissions where possible. The approach emphasizes the utilization of cross-cutting synergies and the combination of thematically separated funding channels. It is recognized as a crucial part of the global climate challenge, especially in combination with forest degradation and competing land use. Accessing international financing for CSA projects requires addressing challenges such as data quality, monitoring systems, institutional capacity, and suitable policy frameworks.

# Climate-Smart Agriculture
An approach that helps to guide actions needed to transform and reorient agricultural systems to effectively support development and ensure food security in a changing climate. CSA aims to tackle three main objectives: sustainably increasing agricultural productivity and incomes; adapting and building resilience to climate change; and reducing and/or removing greenhouse gas emissions, where possible. CSA promotes integrated planning and management at multiple levels and can build on risk assessments and community-based approaches to DRR and adaptation.

# Climate-Smart Agriculture
An integrated approach to managing landscapes—cropland, livestock, forests and fisheries--that addresses the interlinked challenges of food security and climate change. It aims to achieve three main objectives: sustainably increasing agricultural productivity and incomes (food security); adapting and building resilience to climate change (adaptation); and reducing and/or removing greenhouse gas emissions, where possible (mitigation). It is presented as a holistic pathway that brings together multiple areas of expertise and builds on established capacities in Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) and Climate Change Adaptation (CCA). It promotes the application and scaling up of proven practices and follows a landscape approach requiring an understanding of all natural hazards affecting a given territory.
