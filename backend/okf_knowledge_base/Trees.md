# Trees
Integration of trees with crops, fish, and [[Livestock]] is recommended to reduce risk and increase the resilience of farming systems. They play a role in habitat engineering and rehabilitation, providing erosion control, nutrients, shade, and oxygen, and can contribute to carbon sequestration.

# Trees
Trees are mentioned in the context of agroforestry systems and their role in soil management. Tree planting can introduce organic matter at a greater depth in the soil and reduce wind erosion, offering "win-win-win benefits" in most systems. The Quesungual Slash and Mulch Agroforestry System (QSMAS) involves the management of trees and shrubs.

Woody perennial plants, often integrated into agricultural systems. In the context of agroforestry and silvopastoral systems, trees are used to diversify production, buffer farming systems against hazards, reduce heat stress on animals, improve forage supply, and sequester carbon.
