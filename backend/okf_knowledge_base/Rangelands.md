# Rangelands
Rangelands are extensive areas of land, often with specific soil and vegetation characteristics, used for grazing livestock. The text describes participatory rangeland management in the Syrian Arab Republic (Badia) to reduce herders’ vulnerability to climate change and restore productivity. After years of severe drought and intensive grazing, these rangelands were severely degraded, and restoration efforts involved reintroducing native plants to [[Soil Fixation|fix the soil]] and stop [[Sand Encroachment]].

# Rangelands
Rangelands are extensive areas of land used for grazing livestock. The text states that 73 percent of rangelands were estimated to be degraded in the 1990s. Unsustainable management practices like overstocking, overgrazing, and burning of rangelands reduce their productive capacities. Rotational grazing is suggested as a measure to sustain rangeland vegetation quality and reduce wind erosion.

# Rangelands
Extensive natural grasslands, shrublands, woodlands, wetlands, and deserts that are grazed by domestic livestock and wild animals. These areas are characterized by highly diverse vegetation and are managed by pastoralists, such as the Qashqai nomadic pastoralists in Iran, who possess sophisticated knowledge of habitat management and rangeland rehabilitation for maintaining bio-ecological systems.

# Rangelands
Rangelands are extensive areas of land characterized by diverse vegetation, often associated with nomadic and semi-nomadic pastoral systems. In the Islamic Republic of Iran, the highly diverse vegetation of the rangelands has co-evolved with livestock and land management systems of pastoralists like the Qashqai. These pastoralists have developed irreplaceable techniques of habitat management and rangeland rehabilitation for maintaining bio-ecological diversity, including sustainable grazing practices and the use of non-timber products.
