# Native Plants
In the Syrian steppe (or Badia), native plants are being reintroduced as part of participatory rangeland management. These plants are crucial for meeting fodder requirements for livestock, fixing the soil, and stopping sand encroachment. Their reintroduction helps restore degraded ecosystems and reduces herders’ vulnerability to the effects of climatic instability.
