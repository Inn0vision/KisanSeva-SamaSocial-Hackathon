# Negative Coping Strategies
Destructive actions that households may be forced to take to meet short-term needs during a crisis, which ultimately increase their long-term vulnerability. Examples include selling productive assets (livestock, tools), taking children out of school, or over-exploiting natural resources like forests and land. A key function of safety nets is to provide an alternative to these strategies, thereby protecting the capital needed for resilient livelihoods.
