# Mussel
Mussels are bivalve mollusks farmed in aquaculture systems, primarily for human consumption. They are filter feeders, consuming phytoplankton from the water. Two main species are detailed: *Mytilus galloprovincialis* (farmed in Galicia, Spain) and *Mytilus chilensis* (farmed in Southern Chile). Growth is rapid, typically reaching commercial size in 12-18 months. Farming techniques involve suspended lines from floating trays or rafts, or long lines tied to weights on the bottom. Mussel farming is considered carbon-friendly and energy-efficient but is sensitive to climate change threats like red tides, water quality changes, and ocean acidification. Estimated yields range from 60-70 tonnes per hectare (without shell).
