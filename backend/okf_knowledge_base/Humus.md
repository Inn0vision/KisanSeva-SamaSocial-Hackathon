# Humus
Humus is the more stable, recalcitrant (indigestible) fraction of [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]] that is reorganized from original plant and animal material through microbial activity. It is less decomposable and plays a crucial role in increasing the soil’s capacity to store water and sequester carbon from the atmosphere for longer periods. Humus contributes to the long-term stability of [[Soil Organic Carbon (SOC)]] and enhances [[Soil Structure]].
