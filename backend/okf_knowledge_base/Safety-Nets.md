# Safety Nets
A form of social protection comprising programmes supported by the public sector or NGOs that provide transfers (e.g., conditional/unconditional cash transfers, food vouchers, input subsidies, employment programmes) to the poor to address risks, vulnerability, and social exclusion. They are an important strategy for increasing resilience to climate change. Such programmes can stimulate investments in agriculture and livelihood activities, and their effects on risk management and farmer investment behaviour are important factors in designing CSA investment plans.

# Safety Nets
Non-contributory resource transfer programs designed to protect individuals and households from chronic poverty and transient poverty caused by shocks. They function both to prevent people from falling into destitution due to a shock and to help those already in poverty move towards more resilient and productive livelihoods. In the context of CSA, they are instruments for protecting gains and enabling investment in climate-resilient practices. They can involve transfers of cash, vouchers, food, or other goods.
