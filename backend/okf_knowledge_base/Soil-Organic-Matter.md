# Soil Organic Matter
Soil organic matter is a crucial component for soil health and productivity. Manure, when used as organic fertilizer, contributes to the productivity and [[Soil Fertility]] by adding organic matter and nutrients. Increasing soil organic carbon (a key part of soil organic matter) improves both efficiency and resilience, enhancing nutrient and water intake by plants, reducing [[Soil Erosion]], and increasing [[Soil Water Retention]]. It also contributes to climate change mitigation by creating carbon sinks.

# Soil Organic Matter
Soil organic matter (SOM) is a critical component of healthy soil, contributing to soil structure, water retention, nutrient cycling, and overall productivity. Practices like returning agriculture and forestry residues or manure to the soil are essential for increasing SOM. Excessive use of these residues for bioenergy can compete with their role in increasing SOM and thus damage productivity and decrease resilience.

# Soil Organic Matter
The organic component of soil, crucial for soil health and fertility. [[Microorganisms]] play an important role in its decomposition and in the sequestration of carbon within the soil. Practices such as amending soil with organic fertilizers, proper management of crop residues, [[No-tillage agriculture]], and maintaining cover crops promote its accumulation and contribute to [[Carbon sequestration]] and climate change mitigation.
