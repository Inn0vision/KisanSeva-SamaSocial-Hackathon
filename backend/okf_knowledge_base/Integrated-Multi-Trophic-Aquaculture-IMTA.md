# Integrated Multi-Trophic Aquaculture (IMTA)
A coastal aquaculture system linking intensive fish cages or ponds with molluscs and marine plants at a range of scales. It is a climate-smart approach where waste inputs from more intensive aquaculture are used by molluscs and seaweeds grown as byproducts. This system offers potential for enhancing carbon sequestration by managing cage or pond sediments and has scope for expansion as a risk-reduction strategy. It is designed to manage a range of risks and ensure resilience across its interacting components.
