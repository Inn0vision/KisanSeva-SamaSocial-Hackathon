# Risk Assessment
A practical tool for policy formulation, planning, and decision-making in agriculture. It is the combined analysis of potential hazards and existing conditions of vulnerability to determine threats to agriculture ('agricultural risks'). Assessments can be conducted at various spatial scales, from site-specific to national and regional. They are used to inform Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) plans, adaptation-specific plans, Climate-Smart Agriculture (CSA) strategies, land-use planning, development investments, and post-disaster recovery planning. Methodologies vary but advanced approaches follow an all-hazards, all-risks framework to understand cumulative consequences and potential interactions of multiple hazards.
