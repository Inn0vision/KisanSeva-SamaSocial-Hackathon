# Conservation agriculture
A CSA practice involving tillage and residue management to reduce soil disturbance, maintain soil cover, and rotate crops. It is identified as a practice with high potential CSA characteristics, capable of increasing yields over the long run and reducing variability due to reduced erosion and improved soil structure. Weed management and potential waterlogging are noted challenges.
