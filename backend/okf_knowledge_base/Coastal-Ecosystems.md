# Coastal Ecosystems
Coastal ecosystems, including mangroves, coral reefs, wetlands, and sea grass beds, provide critical natural defenses against climate-related hazards. They offer storm protection, flood mitigation, shoreline stabilization, and erosion control. The depletion of these ecosystems, particularly corals and mangroves, reduces nature's defense capacity and aggravates the impact of disasters such as storm surges, hurricanes, and tsunamis. Their conservation and restoration are fundamental to disaster risk reduction and climate change adaptation.
