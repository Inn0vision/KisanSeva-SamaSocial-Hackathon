# Soil Fertility
Soil fertility refers to the capacity of soil to support plant growth and productivity. The text states that manure, as an organic fertilizer, contributes to soil fertility by adding organic matter and nutrients like nitrogen. Nitrogen-fixing leguminous trees, such as [[Faidherbia albida]], are also noted for increasing soil fertility and yields. Agroforestry systems reduce fertilizer costs due to more organic matter in the soil and better use of nutrients by plants, contributing to improved soil fertility.
