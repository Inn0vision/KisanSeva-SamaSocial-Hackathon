# Fish processing
The range of activities after catch or harvest to preserve and add value to aquatic products. This ranges from simple artisanal drying and smoking to highly controlled commercial processing. Small-scale activities include drying/smoking with simple racks and kilns. Commercial processing involves facilities operating to defined health/hygiene standards. Climate change can cause disruptions to resource access and quality. Response strategies include relocating facilities, improving systems and operating procedures, developing a wider range of products, and adding value to improve prices. The adoption of fuel-efficient technology, like the Chorkor oven, is a key response.
