# Soil Health
Soil health is defined by its capacity to provide basic services for supporting plant growth and contributing to the regulation of nutrient, water, carbon, and gaseous cycles. It is widely linked to [[Soil Biodiversity]] and the abundance of diverse organisms within the soil. Key principles of soil health include maintaining [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]], improving [[Soil Structure]], and ensuring proper [[Soil Nutrients]] management. [[Degraded Soils]] are at greater risk from climate change impacts due to poor soil health. Sustainable soil management practices aim to enhance soil health for climate-smart agriculture.

# Soil Health
The continued capacity of soil to function as a vital living ecosystem that sustains plants, animals, and humans. Restoring degraded grassland through [[Grazing Management]] can enhance soil health and water retention, increasing the resilience of grazing systems to climate variability.
