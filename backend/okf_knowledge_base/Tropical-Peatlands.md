# Tropical Peatlands
Tropical peatlands are a specific type of [[Peat Soils]] found in tropical environments. They are identified as "hotspots of carbon emissions" when converted to cropland or plantations. Draining these soils for commercial production systems can lead to ongoing losses of up to 25 tonnes of carbon per hectare per year, emphasizing their critical role in global carbon cycles and the severe impact of their degradation.
