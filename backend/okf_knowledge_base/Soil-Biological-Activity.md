# Soil Biological Activity
Soil biological activity refers to the processes carried out by the diverse community of living organisms within the soil. This activity is fundamental to [[Soil Health]], influencing the decomposition of [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]], the release of [[Soil Nutrients]], and the formation of [[Soil Aggregates]]. Acidity and strong alkalinity in soils can reduce bacterial activity, thereby impairing these vital biological processes. The diversity and abundance of life in the soil are greater than in any other ecosystem, playing a critical role in maintaining soil functions.
