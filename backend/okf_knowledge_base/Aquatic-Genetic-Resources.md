# Aquatic Genetic Resources
Aquatic genetic resources refer to the genetic material of species found in aquatic environments that are valuable for food and agriculture. This includes wild aquatic resources, fish species used in aquaculture (like tilapia and common carp), and other aquatic organisms such as clams and mangroves. Sustainable management and *in situ* conservation are crucial for these common property resources to prevent overexploitation and maintain their gene-pool, allowing fisheries systems and fishers to adapt to new climatic conditions. Efforts include characterizing wild genetic resources and developing improved aquaculture strains.
