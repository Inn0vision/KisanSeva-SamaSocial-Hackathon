# Informal Seed Systems
Informal seed systems encompass all non-certified seed sources, primarily farmers' own saved seeds and seeds obtained through social networks or rural markets. This system is prevalent in developing countries due to its low cost and easy access. Quality is often assured through social principles of trust and reciprocity. However, climate change affects the geographic suitability of varieties, necessitating initiatives to communicate changing conditions and introduce climate-resilient seeds into these networks.
