# Soil Aggregates
Soil aggregates are clusters of individual soil particles (sand, silt, and clay) bound together. Their formation is a key aspect of [[Soil Structure]] and is influenced by [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]] and biological processes. The text discusses the formation of [[Soil Macro-aggregates]] and [[Soil Micro-aggregates]], which are crucial for physically protecting carbon and nitrogen from mineralization and for long-term carbon accumulation. Improving soil structure with organic matter enhances aggregation.
