# Hazard Mapping
The process of delineating geographic areas exposed to a specific type of hazard, such as floods, earthquakes, drought, or tsunamis. These maps indicate the likelihood or probability of occurrence, frequency, and potential severity or magnitude. They are based on historical data and knowledge of past events. Hazard maps are a foundational component of risk assessment and are used for sustainable land-use planning and development investments.
