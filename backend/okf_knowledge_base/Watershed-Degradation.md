# Watershed Degradation
Watershed degradation refers to the deterioration of a river basin's ecological integrity, often caused by deforestation, destruction of protective vegetation, and unsustainable land management practices. This degradation accelerates soil erosion, reduces the soil's water-retention capacity, and makes sloping areas more prone to flooding and landslides. It is a key underlying risk factor that aggravates the impact of climate-related disasters like floods and droughts.
