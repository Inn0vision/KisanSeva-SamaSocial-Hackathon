# Soil Biodiversity
Soil biodiversity refers to the diversity and abundance of life that exists within the soil, which is greater than in any other ecosystem. Billions of different organisms in a handful of soil play a critical role in maintaining [[Soil Health]] and ensuring the soil carries out its functions. Serious losses of soil biodiversity contribute to [[Degraded Soils]]. Greater [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]] content leads to greater soil biodiversity and its activity in breaking down organic matter, making nutrients available, and regulating [[Soil Pests]] and [[Soil Diseases]].
