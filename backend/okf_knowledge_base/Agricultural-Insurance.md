# Agricultural Insurance
A risk management tool that provides financial protection against losses in agricultural production due to adverse weather events and other climate-related risks. It has expanded globally but penetration rates remain low in low-income countries. Both public and private sectors can play a role in expanding coverage. Common public support involves subsidizing premiums, though concerns exist about the effectiveness of such programmes and their potential to incentivize maladaptation.

# Agricultural Insurance
A financial risk management tool designed to protect farmers against losses due to adverse weather events and other agricultural risks. The text indicates that traditional forms of this insurance may not always be feasible in developing countries, highlighting a need for alternative models. It is presented as a mechanism that can increase farmers' ability and willingness to invest in productivity-enhancing measures by mitigating financial risk.
