# Crop Residues
Crop residues are the parts of crops remaining after harvest. They are crucial for bioenergy production (e.g., sugar cane bagasse, wheat straw, rice husks) but also play a vital role in returning carbon to the soil, improving soil management, and increasing soil organic matter. Excessive use for bioenergy can compete with their role in soil health.

# Crop Residues
Crop residues refer to the leftover plant material after harvest. They can be used as feed for animals, reducing waste and improving the efficiency of integrated crop and livestock systems.
