# Mytilus galloprovincialis
The main species of mussel farmed in the Galicia Rias in Spain. It is characterized by very fast growth, often reaching commercial size within one year. The primary production area is the Ria Arousa, which benefits from an upwelling system that provides cold water and nutrients, leading to high phytoplankton production. Farming is conducted on floating rafts, typically 500 m² in size, often family-owned. The estimated annual yield for the Ria Arousa is about 60 tonnes per hectare without shell, with total annual production for Galicia around 250,000 tonnes (with shell).
