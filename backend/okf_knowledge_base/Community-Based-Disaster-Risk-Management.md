# Community-Based Disaster Risk Management
A process developed in the 1980s through which at-risk communities become actively engaged in the identification, analysis, treatment, monitoring, and evaluation of disaster risks to reduce their vulnerabilities and enhance their capacities. It involves communities, including the most vulnerable, in decision-making and implementation. The assessment of hazard exposure and analysis of vulnerabilities and capacities forms the basis for activities to reduce disaster risks.

# Community-Based Disaster Risk Management
A proven participatory method for the assessment of local risks and for guiding local planning. It involves communities in the process of disaster risk assessment, mapping, planning, and implementation of risk reduction measures. It can serve as a vehicle and methodology for promoting Climate-Smart Agriculture (CSA) at local levels. Its widespread application and institutionalization across many countries provide considerable scope for contributing to CSA. Using joint community-based DRR and climate change adaptation approaches creates links with existing local institutional networks and builds coherence within communities and local authorities.
