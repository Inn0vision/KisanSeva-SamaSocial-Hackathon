# Soil Moisture
Soil moisture is critical for crop productivity. Increased temperatures and changes in rainfall patterns can lead to more rapid depletion of soil moisture, causing moisture stress and potentially crop failures, especially in rainfed systems. Better conservation of soil moisture, including through improved soil water holding capacity, is a key adaptation strategy to increase the resilience of farming systems.

# Soil Moisture
A critical characteristic of soil directly impacting agricultural productivity. Climate change is expected to lead to increased variations in soil moisture, resulting in a greater frequency of [[Crop Failures]]. Enhancing the soil's capacity to hold moisture is a key land, water, and crop management option for adaptation and is vital for building resilience against climate variability. On-farm water retention and enhanced infiltration contribute to this.

# Soil Moisture
Soil moisture refers to the water content present in the soil, which is crucial for plant growth. Climate change is expected to reduce the availability of soil moisture in many places due to declining or erratic rainfall, more intense rainfall events, and increased evaporation and transpiration rates. This directly impacts the capacity of soils to make water and nutrients available to plants. Improving water storage in the soil is a key adaptive option for managing soil moisture under changing climate conditions. [[Potassium]] uptake by plants is also positively related to increasing soil moisture.
