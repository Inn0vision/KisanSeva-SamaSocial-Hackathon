# Raised Fields (Waru-Waru)
Raised fields, locally known as waru-waru, are an ancient and productive agricultural technique used in the southern Peruvian Andes. These platforms of soil are surrounded by ditches filled with water. During the day, the water in the trenches warms up, and at night, it releases heat, protecting crops like potatoes and quinoa from frost. The system also maintains soil fertility by allowing silt, sediment, algae, and plant/animal residues in the canals to decay into nutrient-rich muck, which is then added to the raised beds. This technique requires specific expertise and traditional knowledge.
