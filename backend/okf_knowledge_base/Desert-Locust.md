# Desert Locust
The Desert Locust (Schistocerca gregaria) is a highly destructive migratory insect pest known for forming large swarms that can devastate crops and vegetation over vast areas. The text specifically mentions the "Prevention of major Desert Locust upsurges in West and Northwest Africa," highlighting its significant threat to agricultural production and food security in vulnerable regions. Effective management and prevention strategies are crucial to mitigate its impact.

# Desert Locust
The Desert Locust is highlighted as a "highly destructive transboundary plant pest" that threatens livelihoods and food security in over 30 countries. A major upsurge in 2003-2005 caused severe food shortages and significant crop losses, necessitating the application of "chemical pesticides." The text describes the establishment of the Commission for Controlling the Desert Locust in the Western Region (CLCPRO) and the FAO EMPRES Programme for sustainable preventive control, emphasizing early warning systems and rapid interventions.
