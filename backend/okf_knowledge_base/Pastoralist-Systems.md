# Pastoralist Systems
Pastoralism is a land use system and livelihood strategy practiced in drylands, characterized by the mobility of livestock herds to utilize variable rainfall and pasture resources. It is presented as a rational and effective measure against rising temperatures and increased drought incidence. Pastoralist systems are inherently adaptive to climate variability and unpredictability, making them a resilient livelihood option in some of the world's harshest landscapes. However, they face challenges from political marginalization, restrictions on mobility, and inadequate investment in strengthening their coping capacity.
