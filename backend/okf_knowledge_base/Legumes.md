# Legumes
Legumes are valuable for their ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic microbes, which benefits subsequent crops and pasture production when included in crop rotations or forage mixtures. They are important nutritionally, complementing [[Cereals]] by providing proteins and lysine. In livestock feed, legumes can increase the food conversion ratio and decrease methane emissions from ruminants. Despite their benefits, the global area under [[Pulses]] (a type of legume) and their consumption have declined.

# Legumes
Legumes are a type of crop that plays a crucial role in soil fertility due to their symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing _Rhizobia_ bacteria. These bacteria, associated with the roots of legumes, convert atmospheric nitrogen into forms usable by plants (nitrate and ammonium ions). The text notes that legumes generally do not thrive in acidic soils.

# Legumes
An important group of crops that play a crucial role in sustainable agriculture. They are a key source of protein in the diets of many developing countries. In farming systems, legumes are vital for [[Biological Nitrogen Fixation (BNF)]] in the soil, which reduces the need for [[Chemical Fertilizers]] and enhances soil health by reducing [[Soil Pathogens]]. They are effectively used in crop rotation, particularly as cover and catch crops, to suppress the incidence of parasitic weeds like [[Striga]]. Farmers are encouraged to incorporate legumes into crop rotations as part of [[Conservation Agriculture]] practices. Examples mentioned include mucuna, red clover, lucerne, lupine, and jack-beans.
