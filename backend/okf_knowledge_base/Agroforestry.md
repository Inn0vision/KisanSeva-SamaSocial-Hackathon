# Agroforestry
Agroforestry is the practice of integrating trees and shrubs into agricultural systems. It provides multiple benefits, including soil erosion prevention, improved water infiltration, and enhanced biodiversity.

# Agroforestry
A land use management system in which trees or shrubs are grown around or among crops or pastureland. It is a CSA practice highlighted in the Malawi ASWAp that can generate mitigation co-benefits by increasing carbon stocks in above-ground biomass. It can have high up-front investment costs, potentially requiring external finance. Practices include live barriers and community-based dambo management.

# Agroforestry
A land use management system in which trees or shrubs are grown around or among crops or pastureland. It is a biological soil and water conservation technique mentioned as a component of Food-for-Assets programmes. This practice combines agricultural and forestry technologies to create more diverse, productive, profitable, healthy, and sustainable land-use systems, contributing to the resilience and adaptation goals of CSA.
