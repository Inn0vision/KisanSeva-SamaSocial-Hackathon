# Coffee
Coffee is a crop projected to experience dramatic changes in its potential and geographic distribution due to climate change. Traditional production areas, such as São Paulo and Minas Gerais in Brazil, are expected to become unsuitable due to lack of water or high temperatures. Conversely, areas like Paraná, Santa Catarina, and Rio Grande do Sul might see increased production due to reduced frost risk. Globally, the area at low climatic risk for coffee is projected to decrease significantly by 2070.

# Coffee
Coffee shrubs were incorporated into the Kihamba agroforestry system at the end of the 19th century as an ecologically compatible cash crop. In the 1990s, coffee prices plummeted, and [[Pests]] and [[Diseases]] increased, leading to a sharp decline in productivity and profitability. Strategies include conversion to certified organic coffee farming and replacement of ageing trees.

# Coffee
Coffee is a key cash crop discussed in the context of the AdapCC initiative in Nicaragua. Projections indicate that climate change, specifically an average temperature increase of 2.3°C and a decrease in annual rainfall by 100 to 130 mm by 2050, will lead to an overall loss of coffee farming areas. Production will need to move to higher altitudes, and coffee quality is expected to decline. Adaptation measures are being implemented to manage these risks.
