# Peat Soils
Peat soils are organic soils formed from partially decayed vegetation, characterized by high [[Soil Organic Carbon (SOC)]] stocks. They are identified as vulnerable lands, and their conversion to cropland or plantations, especially in tropical environments, makes them "hotspots of carbon emissions." Draining peat soils for commercial production systems can lead to significant ongoing losses of carbon per hectare per year. The text distinguishes between [[Tropical Peatlands]] and [[Boreal Peatlands]].

# Peat Soils
Peat soils are characterized by high organic matter content, often found in wetlands. The text mentions them in the context of greenhouse gas emissions, specifically stating that "Greenhouse gas emissions from managed peat soils" are a concern, implying their role in carbon cycling and climate change.
