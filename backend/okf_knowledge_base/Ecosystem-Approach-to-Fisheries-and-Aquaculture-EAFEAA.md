# Ecosystem Approach to Fisheries and Aquaculture (EAF/EAA)
A holistic strategy for managing capture fisheries and aquaculture that integrates ecological, socio-economic, and institutional dimensions. Its purpose is to plan, develop, and manage fisheries and aquaculture to address societal needs without jeopardizing options for future generations to benefit from aquatic ecosystem goods and services. It strives to balance diverse societal objectives by considering biotic, abiotic, and human components of ecosystems and their interactions, applying an integrated management approach within ecologically meaningful boundaries. Key principles include applying the precautionary approach, using the best available knowledge (scientific or traditional), acknowledging multiple objectives and values of ecosystem services, embracing adaptive management, broadening stakeholder participation with due consideration to gender, ensuring equitable distribution of benefits, and promoting sectoral integration and interdisciplinarity.
