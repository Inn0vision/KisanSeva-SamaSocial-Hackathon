# Soil Macro-aggregates
Soil macro-aggregates are larger aggregates formed by biological processes, such as growing roots, fungal, bacterial, and faunal activity. They play a primary role in mixing fresh [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]] with soil particles and root exudates. These young and unstable macro-aggregates physically protect carbon and nitrogen from mineralization by microbial enzymes. For long-term carbon accumulation, they need to be further stabilized, often leading to the formation of [[Soil Micro-aggregates]] within them. Mechanical soil disturbance, like ploughing, disrupts these important biological processes.
