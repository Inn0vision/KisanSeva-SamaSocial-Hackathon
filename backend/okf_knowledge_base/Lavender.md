# Lavender
Lavender is a medicinal and aromatic plant (MAP) highlighted for its cultivation in the Kashmir region of India. It is grown on 'kandi' land, which is semi-barren, rainfed farmland. Lavender cultivation is highly profitable, with potential yearly profits of up to USD 4,000 per hectare. The crop has a long lifespan of 20 years and requires minimal inputs. It is noted for being highly resilient to climate variability, almost pest-free, and unappealing to grazing cattle. Its cultivation supports the preservation of natural biodiversity by providing a legal alternative to the destructive harvesting of wild plants.
