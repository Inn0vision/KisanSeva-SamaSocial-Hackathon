# Mollusc culture
A subset of aquaculture focusing on the farming of molluscs, such as oysters, clams, and mussels. It includes traditional seabed-based systems and expanded commercial suspended culture systems. These systems are vulnerable to climate change impacts on productivity, substrate quality, spatfall, disease, and predator interactions. Response strategies include adjusting locations, managing substrates, improving health monitoring, and ensuring reliable hatchery seed production.
