# Drylands
Drylands encompass arid and sub-humid regions, covering 47 percent of the Earth’s land surface. Although they have lower mitigation potential per hectare than humid lands, their overall contribution to climate change mitigation could be highly significant due to their vast extent. Many dryland regions are affected by [[Land Degradation]] and are sub-optimally managed, but could rapidly respond to improvements in management practices.

# Drylands
Drylands are areas characterized by a lack of water, which constrains their primary production and nutrient cycling services. They cover 47 percent of the Earth’s land surface and, despite having lower mitigation potential per hectare than humid lands, their overall contribution to climate change mitigation could be highly significant due to their vast extent and often sub-optimally managed status. Many dryland regions are affected by land degradation and could rapidly respond to improvements in management. Water storage in drylands is crucial, and they are vulnerable to salinity.
