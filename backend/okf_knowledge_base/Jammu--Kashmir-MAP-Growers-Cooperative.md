# Jammu & Kashmir MAP Growers’ Cooperative
A producers' cooperative formed in 2009 in Kashmir, India, to support smallholder farmers transitioning from traditional maize cultivation to growing medicinal and aromatic plants (MAPs) like lavender. The cooperative provides planting materials, training, and marketing support to its members. It has established a distillation plant for aromatic oils and created marketing linkages within India and internationally. The cooperative has grown from 30 to 300 members, demonstrating the viability of high-value, climate-resilient crops.
