# Post-harvest Losses
A significant reduction in the quantity and/or quality of food from harvest to consumption. The text highlights this as a major issue in both developed and developing countries, particularly for fruits, vegetables, root crops, fish, milk, and grains. It is a critical focus area for improving resource use and achieving climate-smart agriculture. The text references specific studies on losses in Sub-Saharan Africa and the Near East, and details technologies and interventions designed to reduce these losses, such as improved processing, storage structures, and refrigeration.
