# Climate-smart Agriculture
An integrative approach to address the interlinked challenges of food security and climate change. Its three main pillars are: sustainably increasing agricultural productivity and incomes; adapting and building resilience to climate change; and reducing and/or removing greenhouse gas emissions, where possible. CSA requires changes in farming practices, the adoption of new crop varieties, and shifts in farming schedules. It is heavily dependent on strong institutional support for disseminating information, providing financial services, and coordinating collaborative action among smallholder farmers.
