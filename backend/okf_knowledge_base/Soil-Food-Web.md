# Soil Food Web
The soil food web refers to the complex community of organisms living in the soil, including bacteria, fungi, protozoa, nematodes, arthropods, and earthworms. These organisms play a critical role in maintaining [[Soil Health]] and ensuring the soil carries out its functions. While decomposing [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]], the multitude of organisms in the soil food web release essential [[Soil Nutrients]] (like nitrogen, potassium, calcium, magnesium) necessary for plant growth. The labile fractions of [[Soil Organic Carbon (SOC)]] are important as they fuel the soil food web.
