# Karnataka Watershed Project
A US$ 120 million project in India addressing dryland and hill-farming practices over 0.5 million hectares. Using participatory approaches, it introduced improved technologies and farming practices that led to groundwater recovery, increased crop yields by 24-26%, and increased household incomes by about 53%. A follow-up operation, the Mid-Himalayan watershed project, supports similar activities and is leveraging carbon finance.
