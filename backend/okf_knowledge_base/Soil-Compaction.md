# Soil Compaction
Soil compaction is the reduction in the volume of soil pore spaces, typically caused by heavy machinery, livestock, or human traffic. It is identified as a factor contributing to the vulnerability of [[Degraded Soils]], leading to poor [[Soil Structure]], reduced water infiltration, and hindered root growth, ultimately impacting plant productivity.
