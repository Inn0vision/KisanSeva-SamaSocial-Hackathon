# Soil Texture
Soil texture indicates the relative content of particles of various sizes, specifically sand, silt, and clay, in the soil. It is a fundamental soil property that cannot be changed. Soil texture significantly influences the soil’s capacity to retain water and its ability to retain and exchange nutrients. For example, [[Sandy Soils]] have low water retention capacity, while [[Clay Soils]] and [[Silty Soils]] attract and retain water and nutrients more effectively. Soil porosity depends on both soil structure and texture.
