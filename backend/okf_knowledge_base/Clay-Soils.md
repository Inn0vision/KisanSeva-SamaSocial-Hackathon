# Clay Soils
Clay soils are characterized by a high proportion of clay particles, which attract and retain water and nutrients effectively due to their chemical attraction (electrical charge). They have a higher [[Cation Exchange Capacity]] and a structurally greater potential fertility compared to [[Silty Soils]] and [[Sandy Soils]]. This makes them more productive in terms of moisture and nutrient retention.

# Clay Soils
Clay soils are mentioned alongside loamy soils as being susceptible to compaction due to repetitive hoeing or ploughing, or by heavy machinery and livestock trampling. Improving soil structure with organic matter is beneficial for these soils.
