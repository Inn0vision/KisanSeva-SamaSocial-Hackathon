# Alkaline Soils
Alkaline soils have a pH level ranging from 7 to 9. Strongly alkaline soils are associated with suppressed [[Soil Biological Activity]], resulting in fewer [[Soil Nutrients]] available for plants. They are also at risk of [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]] decomposition, [[Soil Salinization]], soil crusting, and the accumulation of toxic levels of sodium and other minerals. When sodium ions predominate, these soils can become [[Sodic Soils]], presenting challenges due to poor structure that limits water infiltration and drainage.

# Alkaline Soils
Alkaline soils are mentioned as being susceptible to dispersion or crusting, even on gradual slopes, which increases their risk of soil erosion.
