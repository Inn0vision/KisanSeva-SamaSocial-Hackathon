# Kenya Agricultural Carbon Project
A ground-breaking pilot initiative in Kenya implemented by the Swedish NGO Vi-Agroforestry. It aims to illustrate the potential and feasibility of agricultural soil carbon sequestration in the context of carbon markets. It is the first project to sell soil carbon credits in Africa and paves the way for a new approach to carbon accounting methodologies. The project produces carbon credits from Climate-Smart Agriculture activities that are bought by the World Bank’s BioCarbon Fund.
