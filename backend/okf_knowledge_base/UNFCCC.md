# UNFCCC
The United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change. An international environmental treaty that provides the overarching global policy framework for addressing climate change. Its financial mechanism includes operating entities like the Global Environment Facility (GEF) and the Green Climate Fund (GCF), which channel funding for mitigation and adaptation activities, including those related to Climate-Smart Agriculture.

# UNFCCC
Stands for United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change. An international environmental treaty negotiated at the Earth Summit in Rio de Janeiro in 1992. The treaty provides a framework for negotiating specific international treaties (called "protocols") and has a Conference of the Parties (COP) that serves as the supreme body for negotiations. Negotiations on mechanisms like REDD+ are conducted under its auspices.
