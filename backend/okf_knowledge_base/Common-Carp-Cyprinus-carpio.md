# Common Carp (Cyprinus carpio)
The common carp (*Cyprinus carpio*) is described as one of the oldest cultured and most widely domesticated fish globally, with a history of cultivation in China for over 2,500 years. It is highly adaptable in both wild and aquaculture settings. In Hungary, significant genetic improvement programs for common carp began in 1962, leading to the development of productive hybrids and the establishment of a live gene bank with numerous Hungarian and foreign carp strains.
