# Sub-humid Lands
Sub-humid lands are regions characterized by moderate humidity, forming part of the broader category of [[Drylands]]. They cover a significant portion of the Earth's land surface and, despite having lower per-hectare mitigation potential than humid lands, their overall contribution to climate change mitigation can be substantial, especially with improved soil management practices.
