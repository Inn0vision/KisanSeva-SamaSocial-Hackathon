# Soil Organic Carbon (SOC)
Soil Organic Carbon (SOC) is the carbon component of [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]], typically making up about 58 percent of it. SOC is vital for the overall biological resilience of agro-ecosystems and significantly impacts the soil's physical properties (e.g., aggregation, water holding capacity, water infiltration, aeration) and chemical fertility (e.g., nutrient availability). It acts as a sink for atmospheric carbon, contributing to climate change mitigation. Management practices that increase SOC content are emphasized for maintaining productive soils, reducing chemical inputs, and sustaining vital ecosystem functions. The text discusses the stabilization of soil carbon within aggregates and the importance of labile SOC fractions for the soil food web.
