# Fish (Aquatic Species)
Aquatic species, including fish, are significantly affected by changes in freshwater quantity and quality due to climate change. Their important migrations (e.g., spawning and feeding) can be disrupted by altered precipitation patterns. Increased water storage capacity (e.g., larger reservoirs) can promote integration of fish farming through cage culture and enhance fisheries production. Adaptation options include introducing appropriate fish species, adapting fish harvesting calendars, and favoring species with wide environmental tolerances that do not require extensive migrations. Habitat engineering and rehabilitation can also create suitable environments for fish production.
