# Potassium
Potassium is an essential [[Soil Nutrients]] important in various plant physiological processes, and most crops require it in larger amounts than any other mineral element, except [[Nitrogen]]. Its content in different soils is highly variable, depending on the parent material (underlying bedrock or superficial deposits). Unlike nitrogen and phosphorus, potassium availability is more dependent on mineral type and content than on [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]]. Only a small percentage is readily available for plant uptake. Potassium uptake is positively related to increasing (but not exceeding optimum levels of) [[Soil Moisture]], aeration, and temperature. Management practices for potassium vary by crop and soil status, with fertilization expected to yield positive responses when soil test values are low.
