# Climate-smart fisheries and aquaculture
An approach for developing fisheries and aquaculture to respond to global demand for aquatic food in the face of climate change. Key interlinking objectives include: sustainably increasing output productivity/efficiency; reducing vulnerability and building resilience to climate change impacts; and engaging the sector in GHG mitigation processes. It involves adopting best practices, such as those in the FAO Code of Conduct, and using strategies like the EAF/EAA to build system resilience, improve efficient use of resources, support inter-sectoral collaboration, promote integrated monitoring, and avoid maladaptations.
