# Aquifers
Aquifers are underground layers of water-bearing permeable rock, rock fractures, or unconsolidated materials (gravel, sand, or silt) from which groundwater can be extracted. The text mentions "aquifer recharge" as being affected by climate change, with potential reductions in areas like the Mediterranean basin. Aquifers are also vulnerable to salinization, particularly in coastal areas and deltas, due to sea level rise and saltwater intrusion. Groundwater-based irrigation systems rely heavily on aquifers.
