# Peatland
Peatlands are extensive wetlands, such as those on the Ruoergai Plateau, characterized by their high organic matter content (peat). They historically acted as sponges, regulating water supply, preventing erosion, and storing significant amounts of carbon. They also serve as grazing lands but are susceptible to degradation from overgrazing and drainage.

# Peatland
Peatlands are a type of wetland soil important for climate change mitigation through carbon conservation. They are subject to degradation, as observed on the Ruoergai Plateau in Tibet, China. Conservation, rehabilitation, and sustainable use of peatlands are guided by international efforts for climate change mitigation.
