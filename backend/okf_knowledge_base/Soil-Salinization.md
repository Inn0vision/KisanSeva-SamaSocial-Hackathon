# Soil Salinization
Soil salinization is the progressive accumulation of salts (including sodium, phosphorus, calcium, and magnesium) in the surface soil layers, often through capillary action. This process is affected by climate change, with higher temperatures and evaporation rates increasing salt accumulation. While higher rainfall can contribute to leaching out accumulated salts, salinization is a major cause of [[Soil Degradation]] that threatens to limit plant growth and reduce yields on productive agricultural lands. Globally, a significant percentage of irrigated land and groundwater is salt-affected. High levels of soil salinity can only be tolerated if [[Halophytic Plants]] are grown.
