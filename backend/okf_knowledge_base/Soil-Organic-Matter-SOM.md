# Soil Organic Matter (SOM)
Soil Organic Matter (SOM) is the organic fraction of the soil, composed of dead plant and animal materials in various stages of decomposition. It primarily contains [[Soil Organic Carbon (SOC)]] (on average 58 percent) and essential macro- and micronutrients. SOM is crucial for soil health, influencing physical properties (e.g., aggregation, water holding capacity, water infiltration, aeration), chemical fertility (e.g., nutrient availability), and acting as a sink for atmospheric carbon. Higher SOM content leads to greater [[Soil Biodiversity]] and activity, enhancing nutrient availability and water storage capacity. Management practices that increase SOM content are considered "win-win-win" strategies for sequestering carbon, reducing greenhouse gas emissions, and intensifying production.
