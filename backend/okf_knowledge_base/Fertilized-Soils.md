# Fertilized Soils
Fertilized soils are identified as a source of greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, specifically nitrous oxide (N2O), which is pertinent to crop production. The text suggests that reducing the use of or judiciously using inorganic fertilizers can contribute to mitigating climate change by lowering GHG emissions from these soils.

# Fertilized Soils
Soils to which fertilizers have been applied. These soils are identified as a significant source of nitrous oxide (N2O) emissions, a potent greenhouse gas, contributing to agriculture's overall greenhouse gas footprint.
