# Peat Land
Peat land is a type of wetland soil characterized by the accumulation of partially decayed organic matter (peat). The text advises against biofuel development in "high carbon areas" such as primary forests and peat land. This is because disturbing peat lands can release significant amounts of stored carbon, counteracting the climate change mitigation benefits of biofuels. Peat lands are recognized as important natural carbon sinks.
