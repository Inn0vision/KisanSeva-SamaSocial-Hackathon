# Soil Micro-aggregates
Soil micro-aggregates are smaller, more stable aggregates that are formed within [[Soil Macro-aggregates]] during the carbon stabilization process. As macro-aggregates break down, these micro-aggregates are liberated. Their formation and stabilization involve biological factors (such as ageing, root growth, and exudate production) and climate-dependent factors (like wet-dry cycles). They are crucial for the long-term accumulation and protection of [[Soil Organic Carbon (SOC)]].
