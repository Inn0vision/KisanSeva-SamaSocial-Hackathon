# Striga
A parasitic weed, considered one of the biggest obstacles to food production in Africa. It includes about 40 species, with 11 known to parasitize agricultural crops such as [[Maize (Zea mays)]], [[Sorghum (Sorghum bicolor)]], and [[Sugar Cane (Saccharum officinarum)]]. Striga attaches to plant roots, extracting essential nutrients and severely reducing growth, leading to yield losses of up to 100% and affecting the livelihoods of millions.

Striga thrives in temperatures of 30-35°C under semi-arid conditions. Its proliferation is promoted by monoculture of cereals, continuous cropping, and poor agronomic practices like the lack of crop rotation, especially in areas with high human population densities. Striga seeds can remain dormant and viable in the soil for 5-10 years.

Management methods include hand-weeding, organic manure, crop rotation (particularly with [[Legumes]] like [[Mucuna]]), fallow, and the rational use of [[Low Risk Pesticides]]. Climate change, with its potential for increased temperatures and altered rainfall patterns, may influence the geographic distribution and invasive potential of Striga. Damage from Striga is less severe in healthy soils.
