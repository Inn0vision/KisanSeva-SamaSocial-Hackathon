# Zero Tillage
Zero tillage, also known as no-till, is a soil management practice where the soil is left undisturbed from harvest to planting. It is a core component of conservation agriculture, offering significant energy savings (up to 40-50%) primarily by reducing fuel input for soil preparation. Zero tillage, in combination with permanent crop cover and crop rotation, can also sequester carbon.
