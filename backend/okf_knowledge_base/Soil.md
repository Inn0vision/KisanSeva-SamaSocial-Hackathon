# Soil
Soil is a critical component of agricultural systems, acting as a biological carbon capture and storage "sink" and playing an essential role in managing climate change. Key aspects include:
- **Soil Carbon Sequestration:** Enhancing this is a major mitigation potential for agriculture, through practices like reduced tillage, improved grazing management, and restoration of [[Organic Soils]] and [[Degraded Lands]].
- **Soil Organic Matter:** Important for crop growth, water flow regulation, and overall soil health. Improved soil management practices can increase carbon stored in soils. Manure contributes to soil productivity and fertility by adding organic matter and nutrients.
- **Soil Health:** Can be reduced by inappropriate management practices like irrigating without proper drainage.
- **Soil Biota:** Healthy root systems and grazing practices feed soil biota.
- **Soil Forming Processes:** Natural processes promoted by good grazing management.
- **Soil Cover:** Maintaining soil cover is a practice for sustainable crop production intensification.
- **Soil Fertility:** Manure management contributes to soil fertility by adding organic matter and nutrients.
The application of fertilizers, particularly synthetic ones, impacts soils by contributing to nitrous oxide (N2O) emissions.

# Soil
Soil is fundamental for sustained agricultural productivity and the maintenance of vital ecosystem processes. The text extensively discusses various aspects of soil, including its organic matter content (SOM), carbon stocks (SOC), structure, water storage capacity, and vulnerability to degradation. Healthy soils are crucial for resilience to climate change, while degraded soils are at greater risk due to losses of SOM, soil biodiversity, compaction, and increased erosion. Soil management practices, such as minimizing disturbance, optimizing cover, and improving nutrient management, are key to enhancing soil health and productivity.
