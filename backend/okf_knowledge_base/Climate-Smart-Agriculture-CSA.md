# Climate-Smart Agriculture (CSA)
An approach to transforming and reorienting agricultural systems to effectively support development and ensure food security in a changing climate. It aims to achieve three objectives: sustainably increasing agricultural productivity and incomes; adapting and building resilience to climate change; and reducing and/or removing greenhouse gas emissions, where possible. The success of CSA initiatives is highly dependent on understanding and engaging with local institutional contexts.

# Climate-Smart Agriculture (CSA)
A comprehensive agricultural approach that aims to simultaneously achieve three outcomes: 1) sustainably increasing agricultural productivity and incomes; 2) adapting and building resilience to climate change; and 3) reducing and/or removing greenhouse gas emissions, where possible. CSA integrates climate change into the sustainable agricultural development agenda through policies and practices that are resource-efficient, resilient, and low-carbon emitting. It requires greater coherence, coordination and integration between climate change, agricultural development and food security policy processes.

# Climate-Smart Agriculture (CSA)
An integrated approach to agricultural development that aims to achieve three main outcomes: sustainably increasing agricultural productivity and incomes (food security); adapting and building resilience to climate change (adaptation); and reducing and/or removing greenhouse gas emissions, where possible (mitigation). It involves changes in agricultural systems and investments at farm, local institutional, and national levels. Key components include sustainable agricultural intensification, increasing resource use efficiency, and managing risk and uncertainty. The approach requires building an evidence base to identify best options and screening them for their potential adaptation, mitigation, and food security benefits.
