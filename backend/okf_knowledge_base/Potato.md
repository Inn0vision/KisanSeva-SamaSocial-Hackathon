# Potato
A predominant staple food in some countries, with over 5,000 varieties globally. Farming communities in the Andes cultivate more than 175 locally named varieties. Its nutritional value, such as iron content, varies significantly among different varieties (e.g., from 0.14 to 10.4 milligrams per 100 grams edible portion), highlighting the importance of genetic diversity for food security and nutrition.

# Potato
Potato is the fourth most important food crop globally after rice, wheat, and maize. It is vital to the diets and livelihoods of millions of people. Climate change is expected to increase plant diseases and pests that affect potato production, such as the expansion of late blight into previously unaffected areas. Increases in temperature also put additional pressure on the potato's wild relatives, with 16-22 percent of all wild potato species threatened with extinction by 2055. These wild relatives serve as critical gene pools for breeding new varieties. Conservation efforts, including community gene banks and the repatriation of native varieties, are crucial for maintaining genetic diversity and building resilience.
