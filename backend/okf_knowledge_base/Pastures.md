# Pastures
Irrigated pastures are important in some areas and their significance is growing due to increasing demand for [[Animal Feed]]. Better pasture management, combined with feed additives, can substantially reduce [[Methane Emissions]] from [[Ruminants]].

# Pastures
Pastures are lands used for grazing livestock. The text notes that pastures established on cleared Amazon rainforest emit between 8 and 12 tonnes of carbon per hectare. In grazing systems, Soil Organic Matter (SOM) can be increased through controlled grazing, which reduces vegetation degradation and restores grassland diversity. Pasture cropping, where an annual crop is grown out-of-phase with perennial pasture, is also mentioned as a practice that builds soil at higher rates.
