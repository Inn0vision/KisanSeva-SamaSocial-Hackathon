# Mytilus chilensis
The most common farmed mussel species in Chile. Farming is conducted using long-line systems, where a 'mother line' (approx. 100 m in length) is held by floats and anchored to the bottom, with many vertical lines hanging from it. Farms operate in protected bays, channels, or fjords at depths of 10-40 m, with a density of 10-12 long lines per hectare. Estimated yield ranges from 60 to 70 tonnes per hectare without shell. In 2011, Chile produced 221,000 tonnes of mussels. The industry generates significant local direct and indirect employment, particularly in processing.
