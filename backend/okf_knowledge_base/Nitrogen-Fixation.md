# Nitrogen Fixation
Nitrogen fixation is the process by which atmospheric nitrogen (N2), which plants cannot directly use, is converted into nitrate and ammonium ions in the soil. This vital process is carried out by certain soil microorganisms, including symbiotic _Rhizobia_ bacteria associated with the roots of [[Legumes]], and non-symbiotic bacteria like _Clostridium_ and _Azoterbacter_ that are free-living in the soil. Nitrogen fixation is crucial for making [[Nitrogen]] available for [[Plant Growth]] and contributes significantly to [[Soil Fertility]].
