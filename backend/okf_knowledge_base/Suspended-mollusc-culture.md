# Suspended mollusc culture
An expanded, commercial form of mollusc aquaculture conducted in coastal zones across many latitudes for commercial markets. This system is vulnerable to greater ecosystem changes and extreme events. Response strategies to climate disruptions include improving monitoring, strengthening farming systems, adjusting locations and management practices, and ensuring reliable hatchery seed production. In cases of major disruption, a shift to suspended culture systems from seabed-based systems is a possible response.
