# Soil Carbon
Soil carbon refers to the carbon stored within the soil. Increasing the carbon content of soils is a key mitigation co-benefit of many climate-smart agricultural and land management systems. Improved grazing management can lead to an increase of soil carbon stocks, and [[Peatlands]] are noted as containing 30 percent of the world's soil carbon.

# Soil Carbon
Soil carbon refers to the organic carbon stored in soil, which is crucial for soil health and plays a significant role in climate change mitigation through carbon sequestration. Impacts of grazing intensity on soil carbon in alpine meadows have been studied.
