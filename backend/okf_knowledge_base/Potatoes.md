# Potatoes
Potatoes are a significant native crop, particularly in the southern Peruvian Andes, where farmers cultivate several varieties, often using traditional "raised fields" (waru-waru) for temperature regulation and soil fertility. Peru's Potato Park, a 12,000-hectare reserve near Cusco, is dedicated to conserving the region's potato biodiversity, including many varieties that had disappeared from farmers' fields but were repatriated from gene banks. This conservation effort supports local communities in adapting to climate change, as farmers experiment with different varieties at higher altitudes due to warming climates.
