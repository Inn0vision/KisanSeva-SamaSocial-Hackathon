# Soil Nutrients
Soil nutrients are essential elements in the soil that support plant growth. Nutrient recycling is a supporting ecosystem service. The assessment approach for landscape planning considers the status and dynamics of land resources, including soil nutrients and [[Organic Matter]], as determinants of productivity and ecosystem resilience.

# Soil Nutrients
Soil nutrients are essential mineral elements required for plant growth, including nitrogen, potassium, calcium, magnesium, and phosphorus. These nutrients are released into the soil by the multitude of organisms in the [[Soil Food Web]] as they decompose [[Soil Organic Matter (SOM)]]. Many exist as positively charged ions (cations) and are retained by the negative charges on clay particles and SOM, forming a nutrient reserve. Plants obtain nutrients through processes like [[Cation Exchange Capacity]]. The availability of these nutrients is influenced by [[Soil pH]], [[Soil Moisture]], aeration, and temperature. Boosting nutrient management is a key principle for climate change adaptation and mitigation.
