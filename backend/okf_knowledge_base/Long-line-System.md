# Long-line System
An aquaculture technique used for mussel farming, particularly in Chile. It involves a ‘mother line’ normally about 100 m in length that is held by floats and tied to the bottom. Many vertical lines hang from the mother line, to which mussel seeds attach. Farming density can range from 10 to 12 long lines per ha in protected water bodies.
