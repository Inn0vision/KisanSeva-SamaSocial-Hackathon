# Wetland Ecosystems
Wetland ecosystems are described as rich in biodiversity and provide numerous services that strengthen the resilience of agricultural systems. The rice-fish agricultural system, for instance, thrives in these environments. The Kuttanad Wetland in India is a specific example of a below-sea-level farming system transformed from swamps, supporting a wide variety of plants and aquatic life, and contributing to the conservation of biodiversity and ecosystem services.
