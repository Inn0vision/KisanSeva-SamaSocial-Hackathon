# Non-Timber Forest Products
Non-timber products encompass a variety of valuable resources derived from rangelands and ecosystems, relied upon for subsistence and occasionally sold at markets. Examples include gums, medicinal and veterinary plants, vegetable dyes, mushrooms, and other edible herbs and fruits. The Qashqai nomadic pastoralists in Iran possess extensive traditional knowledge regarding the names and properties of botanical species, including their value for medicine, food, feed, manufacturing, and their place in the ecosystem, emphasizing sustainable use practices.
